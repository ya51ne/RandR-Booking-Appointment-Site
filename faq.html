Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our wellness treatments

Multivitamin Shots

What are multivitamin shots and how do they work?

+

Multivitamin shots are intramuscular injections that deliver essential vitamins directly into your bloodstream, bypassing the digestive system for maximum absorption. This method ensures nearly 100% bioavailability compared to oral supplements which may only be 10-20% absorbed.

How often should I get vitamin injections?

+

The frequency depends on your individual needs and vitamin levels. Generally, B12 shots can be given weekly or monthly, while other vitamins may be administered less frequently. We'll discuss a personalised schedule based on your health goals during your consultation.

Are vitamin shots painful?

+

Most clients experience minimal discomfort. The injection feels like a quick pinch and is over within seconds. We use fine needles and proper injection techniques to minimize any discomfort.

What are the benefits of B12 injections?

+

B12 injections can help increase energy levels, improve mood, support brain function, boost metabolism, and aid in red blood cell formation. They're particularly beneficial for those with B12 deficiency, vegetarians, or people with absorption issues.

Who should avoid vitamin injections?

+

Pregnant or breastfeeding women, people with certain medical conditions, or those taking specific medications should consult with their healthcare provider first. We'll review your medical history during consultation to ensure safety.

Massage Therapy

What should I expect during my first massage?

+

During your first visit, we'll discuss your health history, areas of concern, and treatment goals. You'll be provided with privacy to undress to your comfort level and will be properly draped throughout the session. The massage will be tailored to your specific needs and preferences.

What is the difference between Swedish massage and lymphatic drainage?

+

Swedish massage uses long, flowing strokes to promote relaxation and improve circulation. Lymphatic drainage uses gentle, rhythmic movements to stimulate the lymphatic system, helping to reduce swelling and boost immune function. Both are therapeutic but serve different purposes.

How often should I get a massage?

+

This depends on your individual needs and goals. For general wellness, monthly massages are beneficial. For specific issues or stress management, weekly or bi-weekly sessions may be recommended initially. We'll discuss the best schedule for you.

What are the benefits of Indian Head Massage?

+

Indian Head Massage focuses on the head, neck, and shoulders to relieve tension, improve circulation, reduce stress, and promote hair health. It can help with headaches, insomnia, and mental fatigue while providing deep relaxation.

Should I do anything special before or after my massage?

+

Arrive hydrated and avoid large meals beforehand. After your massage, drink plenty of water, rest when possible, and avoid strenuous activities for a few hours to maximize the benefits of your treatment.

Cupping/Hijama

What is the difference between dry and wet cupping?

+

Dry cupping uses suction to draw skin and tissue into cups without breaking the skin. Wet cupping (Hijama) involves small, superficial incisions before cupping to draw out small amounts of blood, which is believed to remove toxins and improve circulation.

Does cupping hurt?

+

Most people find cupping to be quite comfortable. You may feel a tight pulling sensation, but it shouldn't be painful. Wet cupping involves minimal discomfort from small incisions. Any marks left by cupping typically fade within a few days to a week.

What conditions can cupping help with?

+

Cupping may help with muscle tension, pain relief, improved circulation, reduced inflammation, stress relief, and general wellness. Many people find it beneficial for back pain, headaches, and respiratory issues.

How should I prepare for a cupping session?

+

Eat a light meal 2-3 hours before your session, stay hydrated, and wear comfortable clothing that allows access to treatment areas. Avoid alcohol and caffeine on the day of treatment.

Who should avoid cupping therapy?

+

Cupping should be avoided by pregnant women, people with skin conditions, blood disorders, or those taking blood-thinning medications. We'll review your medical history to ensure cupping is safe for you.
